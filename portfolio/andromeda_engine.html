Andromeda Engine

Resume

Andromeda Engine is a graphics and physics development engine, written in C++, that uses DirectX 11 and OpenGL as rendering backends. These two popular graphics APIs enable broad compatibility and performance on both Windows and cross-platform systems. The engine's flexibility is further enhanced by the integration of other advanced APIs such as PhysX, for accurate and realistic physics simulation, and OpenAL, which provides a high-quality three-dimensional sound system.

In addition to its desktop focus, Andromeda Engine has been successfully ported to Xbox through the use of the Universal Windows Platform (UWP).

Engine Features

Andromeda Engine is a development engine that offers advanced features for creating graphics, lighting and sound effects. With various rendering options, dynamic lighting and shadows, as well as integration with Nvidia PhysX for physics and OpenAL for 3D sound.

Here is a list of the implemented features:

  • Rendering
    • Forward Rendering
    • Deferred Rendering
    • Deferred Rendering with Physically Based Rendering (PBR)
  • Lighting
    • Ambient Light
    • Directional Light
    • Point Light
    • Spot Light
  • Shadow Mapping with all lights
  • Nvidia PhysX integrated
  • Sound integrated with OpenAL
  • Multithreading
